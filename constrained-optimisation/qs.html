Optimisation Problems

  1. Given the function \(z=x^2+2xy+4y+10\) with the constraint \(x+y=100\), optimise the function \(z\) subject to the constraint by:

    1. Writing down the Lagrangian function.
    2. Writing down the three first-order conditions.
    3. Solving the first-order conditions to find the optimal \(x\), \(y\), and the value of \(z\).
    Show Solution

    Lagrangian:

    $$ \mathcal{L}(x, y, \lambda) = x^2+2xy+4y+10+\lambda(100-x-y) $$

    FOCs:

    $$ \begin{align*} \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial x} &= 2x+2y-\lambda=0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial y} &= 2x+4-\lambda=0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} &= 100-x-y=0 \end{align*} $$

    Make \(\lambda\) the subject of the first two FOCs and set them equal:

    $$ 2x+2y = 2x+4 \implies 2y=4 \implies y=2 $$

    Substitute into constraint:

    $$ x+2=100 \implies x=98 $$

    Substitute into \(z\):

    $$ z = (98)^2 + 2(98)(2) + 4(2) + 10 = 10014 $$

    Optimal Solution: \(x=98\), \(y=2\), \(z=10014\).

  2. Given the function \(z=12x+4y\) with the constraint \(x^{0.3}y^{0.7}=1000\), optimise the function \(z\) subject to the constraint.

    Show Solution

    Lagrangian:

    $$ \mathcal{L} = 12x+4y+\lambda(1000-x^{0.3}y^{0.7}) $$

    FOCs:

    $$ \begin{align*} \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial x} &= 12 - 0.3\lambda x^{-0.7}y^{0.7} = 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial y} &= 4 - 0.7\lambda x^{0.3}y^{-0.3} = 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} &= 1000-x^{0.3}y^{0.7} = 0 \end{align*} $$

    Rearrange and divide:

    $$ \frac{12}{4} = \frac{0.3\lambda x^{-0.7}y^{0.7}}{0.7\lambda x^{0.3}y^{-0.3}} \implies 3 = \frac{3y}{7x} \implies y=7x $$

    Substitute into constraint and solve:

    $$ \begin{align*} x^{0.3}(7x)^{0.7} &= 1000 \\ 7^{0.7}x &= 1000 \\ x &= \frac{1000}{7^{0.7}} \approx 256.1 \\ y &= 7x \approx 1792.8 \\ z &= 12x+4y \approx 10244 \end{align*} $$

    Optimal Solution: \(x \approx 256.1\), \(y \approx 1792.8\), \(z \approx 10244\).

  3. Given the function \(z=x^{0.6}y^{0.4}\) with the constraint \(5x+8y=120\), optimise the function \(z\) subject to the constraint.

    Show Solution

    Lagrangian:

    $$ \mathcal{L} = x^{0.6}y^{0.4}+\lambda(120-5x-8y) $$

    FOCs:

    $$ \begin{align*} \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial x} &= 0.6x^{-0.4}y^{0.4} - 5\lambda = 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial y} &= 0.4x^{0.6}y^{-0.6} - 8\lambda = 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} &= 120-5x-8y=0 \end{align*} $$

    Rearrange and divide:

    $$ \frac{0.6x^{-0.4}y^{0.4}}{0.4x^{0.6}y^{-0.6}} = \frac{5\lambda}{8\lambda} \implies \frac{3y}{2x} = \frac{5}{8} \implies y = \frac{5}{12}x $$

    Substitute into constraint:

    $$ \begin{align*} 5x + 8\left(\frac{5}{12}x\right) &= 120 \\ 5x + \frac{10}{3}x &= 120 \\ \frac{25}{3}x &= 120 \\ x &= 14.4 \\ y &= 6 \\ z &= 10.1 \end{align*} $$

  4. A consumer has the utility function \(U(x,y)=x^{0.5}y^{0.5}\). Income is 100, \(p_x=2\), and \(p_y=4\).

    1. Write down the budget constraint for the consumer.
    2. Maximise the customer's utility, subject to their budget constraint.
    Show Solution

    (a) Budget Constraint: \( 2x+4y=100 \)

    (b) Utility Maximisation

    Lagrangian:

    $$ \mathcal{L} = x^{0.5}y^{0.5} + \lambda(100 - 2x - 4y) $$

    FOCs:

    $$ \begin{align*} \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial x} &= 0.5x^{-0.5}y^{0.5} - 2\lambda = 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial y} &= 0.5x^{0.5}y^{-0.5} - 4\lambda = 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} &= 100-2x-4y=0 \end{align*} $$

    Rearrange and divide:

    $$ \frac{0.5x^{-0.5}y^{0.5}}{0.5x^{0.5}y^{-0.5}} = \frac{2\lambda}{4\lambda} \implies \frac{y}{x} = \frac{1}{2} \implies x=2y $$

    Substitute into constraint and solve:

    $$ \begin{align*} 2(2y) + 4y &= 100 \\ 8y &= 100 \\ y &= 12.5 \\ x &= 25 \\ U &\approx 17.7 \end{align*} $$

  5. A firm's production is \(Q=L^{\alpha}K^{\beta}\). The cost of labour is 10 and capital is 20. Total output is constrained to 100 units.

    1. Use the Lagrangian method to show that minimum cost occurs when \( L = \frac{2\alpha K}{\beta} \) to give expressions for \(L^*\) and \(K^*\).
    2. Find the optimal values and minimum cost for (i) \(\alpha=\beta=0.5\) and (ii) \(\alpha=0.3, \beta=0.7\).
    Show Solution

    (a) Deriving Optimal Inputs

    Minimize \(C=10L+20K\) subject to \(L^{\alpha}K^{\beta}=100\).

    $$ \mathcal{L} = 10L+20K+\lambda(100 - L^{\alpha}K^{\beta}) $$

    FOCs:

    $$ \begin{align*} \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial L} &= 10 - \lambda \alpha L^{\alpha-1}K^{\beta} = 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial K} &= 20 - \lambda \beta L^{\alpha}K^{\beta-1} = 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} &= 100 - L^{\alpha}K^{\beta} = 0 \end{align*} $$

    Rearrange and divide:

    $$ \frac{10}{20} = \frac{\lambda \alpha L^{\alpha-1}K^{\beta}}{\lambda \beta L^{\alpha}K^{\beta-1}} \implies \frac{1}{2} = \frac{\alpha K}{\beta L} \implies L = \frac{2\alpha K}{\beta} $$

    Substitute into constraint and solve:

    $$ \begin{align*} \left( \frac{2 \alpha K}{\beta} \right )^{\alpha}K^{\beta} &= 100 \\ \left( \frac{2 \alpha}{\beta} \right )^{\alpha}K^{\alpha + \beta} &= 100 \\ K^{\alpha +\beta} &= 100 \left (\frac{2 \alpha}{\beta} \right )^{-\alpha} \\ K=K^* &= \left( 100 \left(\frac{2\alpha}{\beta} \right)^{-\alpha} \right)^{\frac{1}{\alpha+\beta}} \end{align*} $$ $$ \begin{align*} L &= \frac{2\alpha K}{\beta} \\ L=L^* &= \left( 100 \left(\frac{2\alpha}{\beta} \right)^{\beta} \right)^{\frac{1}{\alpha+\beta}} \end{align*} $$

    (b) Finding Optimal Values

    (i) \(\alpha=\beta=0.5\):

    $$ \begin{align*} K^* &= \left( 100 \left(\frac{2 \cdot 0.5}{0.5}\right)^{-0.5} \right)^{1} \approx 70.7 \\ L^* &= \left( 100 \left(\frac{2 \cdot 0.5}{0.5}\right)^{0.5} \right)^{1} \approx 141.4 \\ C^* &= 10(141.4) + 20(70.7) = 2828 \end{align*} $$

    (ii) \(\alpha=0.3, \beta=0.7\):

    $$ \begin{align*} K^* &\approx 104.7 \\ L^* &\approx 89.8 \\ C^* &= 2992 \end{align*} $$

  6. A monopoly faces a demand function \(P=50-Q\) and a cost function \(C=10+2Q\). Find the maximum profit \(\Pi\) and the corresponding values of \(P\) and \(Q\).

    Show Solution

    Maximise \(\Pi=PQ-C\) subject to \(P=50-Q\).

    $$ \Pi = PQ - (10+2Q) $$

    Lagrangian:

    $$ \mathcal{L} = PQ - 10 - 2Q + \lambda(50 - Q - P) $$

    FOCs:

    $$ \begin{align*} \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial P} &= Q - \lambda = 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial Q} &= P - 2 - \lambda = 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} &= 50 - Q - P = 0 \end{align*} $$

    From the first two FOCs, \(Q = P - 2\).

    Substitute into constraint and solve:

    $$ \begin{align*} P &= 50 - (P-2) \\ 2P &= 52 \\ P &= 26 \\ Q &= 24 \\ \Pi &= 566 \end{align*} $$

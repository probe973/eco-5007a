Optimisation Problems

  1. Given the function \(z=x^2+2xy+4y+10\) with the constraint \(x+y=100\), optimise the function \(z\) subject to the constraint by:

    1. Writing down the Lagrangian function.
    2. Writing down the three first-order conditions.
    3. Solving the first-order conditions to find the optimal \(x\), \(y\), and the value of \(z\).
    Show Solution

    (a) The Lagrangian Function

    $$ \mathcal{L}(x, y, \lambda) = x^2+2xy+4y+10+\lambda(100-x-y) $$

    (b) First-Order Conditions (FOCs)

    $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial x} = 2x+2y-\lambda=0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial y} = 2x+4-\lambda=0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} = 100-x-y=0 $$

    (c) Solving for Optimal Values

    From the first two FOCs, we can isolate \(\lambda\):

    $$ 2x+2y = \lambda $$ $$ 2x+4 = \lambda $$

    Setting these equal gives:

    $$ 2x+2y = 2x+4 \implies 2y=4 \implies y=2 $$

    Substitute \(y=2\) into the constraint (the third FOC):

    $$ 100-x-2=0 \implies x=98 $$

    Substitute the optimal values of \(x\) and \(y\) back into the objective function \(z\):

    $$ z = (98)^2 + 2(98) + 4(2) + 10 = 9818 $$

    Optimal Solution: \(x=98\), \(y=2\), \(z=9818\).

  2. Given the function \(z=12x+4y\) with the constraint \(x^{0.3}y^{0.7}=1000\), optimise the function \(z\) subject to the constraint.

    Show Solution

    The Lagrangian is:

    $$ \mathcal{L} = 12x+4y+\lambda(1000-x^{0.3}y^{0.7}) $$

    The FOCs are:

    $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial x} = 12 - 0.3\lambda x^{-0.7}y^{0.7} = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial y} = 4 - 0.7\lambda x^{0.3}y^{-0.3} = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} = 1000-x^{0.3}y^{0.7} = 0 $$

    Isolating the terms with \(\lambda\):

    $$ 12 = 0.3\lambda x^{-0.7}y^{0.7} $$ $$ 4 = 0.7\lambda x^{0.3}y^{-0.3} $$

    Dividing the first equation by the second:

    $$ \frac{12}{4} = \frac{0.3\lambda x^{-0.7}y^{0.7}}{0.7\lambda x^{0.3}y^{-0.3}} \implies 3 = \frac{3}{7} \frac{y}{x} $$

    Solving for \(y\):

    $$ 21x = 3y \implies y=7x $$

    Substitute into the constraint:

    $$ x^{0.3}(7x)^{0.7} = 1000 \implies x^{0.3} \cdot 7^{0.7} \cdot x^{0.7} = 1000 $$ $$ 7^{0.7}x = 1000 \implies x = \frac{1000}{7^{0.7}} \approx 256.1 $$

    Now find \(y\) and \(z\):

    $$ y = 7x \approx 7(256.1) \approx 1792.8 $$ $$ z = 12(256.1) + 4(1792.8) \approx 10244 $$

    Optimal Solution: \(x \approx 256.1\), \(y \approx 1792.8\), \(z \approx 10244\).

  3. Given the function \(z=x^{0.6}y^{0.4}\) with the constraint \(5x+8y=120\), optimise the function \(z\) subject to the constraint.

    Show Solution

    The Lagrangian is:

    $$ \mathcal{L} = x^{0.6}y^{0.4}+\lambda(120-5x-8y) $$

    The FOCs are:

    $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial x} = 0.6x^{-0.4}y^{0.4} - 5\lambda = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial y} = 0.4x^{0.6}y^{-0.6} - 8\lambda = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} = 120 - 5x - 8y = 0 $$

    Isolating the terms with \(\lambda\) and dividing gives:

    $$ \frac{0.6x^{-0.4}y^{0.4}}{0.4x^{0.6}y^{-0.6}} = \frac{5\lambda}{8\lambda} \implies \frac{3}{2}\frac{y}{x} = \frac{5}{8} $$

    Solving for \(y\):

    $$ \frac{y}{x} = \frac{5}{8} \cdot \frac{2}{3} = \frac{10}{24} = \frac{5}{12} \implies y = \frac{5}{12}x $$

    Substitute into the constraint:

    $$ 5x + 8\left(\frac{5}{12}x\right) = 120 \implies 5x + \frac{10}{3}x = 120 $$ $$ \frac{15x+10x}{3} = 120 \implies \frac{25}{3}x = 120 \implies x = \frac{120 \cdot 3}{25} = 14.4 $$

    Now find \(y\) and \(z\):

    $$ y = \frac{5}{12}(14.4) = 6 $$ $$ z = (14.4)^{0.6}(6)^{0.4} \approx 10.1 $$

    Optimal Solution: \(x = 14.4\), \(y = 6\), \(z \approx 10.1\).

  4. A consumer has the utility function \(U(x,y)=x^{0.5}y^{0.5}\). Income is 100, \(p_x=2\), and \(p_y=4\).

    1. Write down the budget constraint for the consumer.
    2. Maximise the customer's utility, subject to their budget constraint.
    Show Solution

    (a) Budget Constraint

    $$ 2x+4y=100 $$

    (b) Utility Maximisation

    The Lagrangian is:

    $$ \mathcal{L} = x^{0.5}y^{0.5} + \lambda(100 - 2x - 4y) $$

    The FOCs are:

    $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial x} = 0.5x^{-0.5}y^{0.5} - 2\lambda = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial y} = 0.5x^{0.5}y^{-0.5} - 4\lambda = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} = 100-2x-4y=0 $$

    Dividing the first two conditions gives:

    $$ \frac{0.5x^{-0.5}y^{0.5}}{0.5x^{0.5}y^{-0.5}} = \frac{2\lambda}{4\lambda} \implies \frac{y}{x} = \frac{1}{2} \implies x=2y $$

    Substitute into the budget constraint:

    $$ 2(2y) + 4y = 100 \implies 8y = 100 \implies y = 12.5 $$

    Now find \(x\) and \(U\):

    $$ x = 2(12.5) = 25 $$ $$ U = (25)^{0.5}(12.5)^{0.5} \approx 17.7 $$

    Optimal Solution: The consumer should buy 25 units of good x and 12.5 units of good y for a maximum utility of approximately 17.7.

  5. A firm's production is \(Q=L^{\alpha}K^{\beta}\). The cost of labour is 10 and capital is 20. Total output is constrained to 100 units.

    1. Use the Lagrangian method to derive the optimal input functions \(L^*\) and \(K^*\).
    2. Find the optimal values and minimum cost for (i) \(\alpha=\beta=0.5\) and (ii) \(\alpha=0.3, \beta=0.7\).
    Show Solution

    (a) Deriving Optimal Inputs

    We want to minimise cost \(C=10L+20K\) subject to the constraint \(L^{\alpha}K^{\beta}=100\).

    The Lagrangian is:

    $$ \mathcal{L} = 10L+20K+\lambda(100 - L^{\alpha}K^{\beta}) $$

    The FOCs are:

    $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial L} = 10 - \lambda \alpha L^{\alpha-1}K^{\beta} = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial K} = 20 - \lambda \beta L^{\alpha}K^{\beta-1} = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} = 100 - L^{\alpha}K^{\beta} = 0 $$

    Dividing the first two conditions:

    $$ \frac{10}{20} = \frac{\lambda \alpha L^{\alpha-1}K^{\beta}}{\lambda \beta L^{\alpha}K^{\beta-1}} \implies \frac{1}{2} = \frac{\alpha K}{\beta L} \implies L = \frac{2\alpha K}{\beta} $$

    Substitute this into the production constraint:

    $$ \left(\frac{2\alpha K}{\beta}\right)^{\alpha} K^{\beta} = 100 \implies \left(\frac{2\alpha}{\beta}\right)^{\alpha} K^{\alpha+\beta} = 100 $$ $$ K^{\alpha+\beta} = 100 \left(\frac{2\alpha}{\beta}\right)^{-\alpha} \implies K^* = \left( 100 \left(\frac{2\alpha}{\beta}\right)^{-\alpha} \right)^{\frac{1}{\alpha+\beta}} $$

    Substitute \(K^*\) back into the expression for \(L\):

    $$ L = \frac{2\alpha}{\beta} K = \left(\frac{2\alpha}{\beta}\right) \left( 100 \left(\frac{2\alpha}{\beta} \right)^{-\alpha} \right)^{\frac{1}{\alpha+\beta}} $$ $$ L^* = \left( 100 \left(\frac{2\alpha}{\beta}\right)^{1-\alpha} \right)^{\frac{1}{\alpha+\beta}} $$

    (b) Finding Optimal Values

    (i) Case \(\alpha=0.5, \beta=0.5\):

    $$ K^* = \left(100 \left(\frac{2 \cdot 0.5}{0.5}\right)^{-0.5}\right)^{\frac{1}{1}} \approx 70.7 $$ $$ L^* = \left(100 \left(\frac{2 \cdot 0.5}{0.5}\right)^{1-0.5}\right)^{\frac{1}{1}} \approx 141.4 $$ $$ C_{min} = 10(141.4) + 20(70.7) = 2828 $$

    (ii) Case \(\alpha=0.3, \beta=0.7\):

    $$ K^* = \left(100 \left(\frac{2 \cdot 0.3}{0.7}\right)^{-0.3}\right)^{\frac{1}{1}} \approx 104.7 $$ $$ L^* = \left(100 \left(\frac{2 \cdot 0.3}{0.7}\right)^{1-0.3}\right)^{\frac{1}{1}} \approx 89.8 $$ $$ C_{min} = 10(89.8) + 20(104.7) = 2992 $$

  6. A monopoly faces a demand function \(P=50-Q\) and a cost function \(C=10+2Q\). Find the maximum profit \(\Pi\) and the corresponding values of \(P\) and \(Q\).

    Show Solution

    The objective is to maximise profit \(\Pi = PQ - C\). $$ \Pi = PQ - (10+2Q) $$ The constraint is the demand function: \(P=50-Q\).

    The Lagrangian is:

    $$ \mathcal{L} = PQ - 10 - 2Q + \lambda(50 - Q - P) $$

    The FOCs are:

    $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial P} = Q - \lambda = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial Q} = P - 2 - \lambda = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} = 50 - Q - P = 0 $$

    From the first two FOCs, we get:

    $$ Q = \lambda $$ $$ P - 2 = \lambda $$ $$ \implies Q = P - 2 $$

    Substitute this into the constraint:

    $$ 50 - (P-2) - P = 0 \implies 52 - 2P = 0 \implies P = 26 $$

    Now find \(Q\) and \(\Pi\):

    $$ Q = 26 - 2 = 24 $$ $$ \Pi = (26)(24) - (10 + 2(24)) = 624 - 58 = 566 $$

    Optimal Solution: The monopoly should set a price of \(P=26\) and sell a quantity of \(Q=24\) to achieve a maximum profit of \(566\).

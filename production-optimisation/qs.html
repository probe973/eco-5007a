1. A company produces an item that can be manufactured on one of two machines (named A and B). Their primary concern is going to be costs. If $q_A$ are produced on machine A, the cost of production is $$TC_A=10+20q_A$$ If $q_B$ are produced on machine B, the cost of production is $$TC_B=2+q_B^{2}$$ The company are about to produce 100 of the item.

    1. What is the total cost if all are produced on machine A?

    2. What is the total cost if all are produced on machine B?

    3. What combination of machine A and machine B would minimize the total cost, and what would that total cost be?

    Show Solution
    1. All A: $TC=10+20\times 100=2010$
    2. All B: $TC=2+100^2=10002$

    3. Minimise costs $TC_A+TC_B$ subject to $q_A+q_B=100$:

      Lagrangian: $$L=10+20q_A+2+q_B^{2}+\lambda(100-q_A-q_B)$$

      FOC: \begin{align} L_{q_A}&=20-\lambda=0\\ L_{q_B}&=2q_B-\lambda=0\\ L_{\lambda}&=100-q_A-q_B=0 \end{align} From FOC equation (1) we have $$\lambda=20$$ Putting this into FOC equation (2) gives $$2q_B=20 \implies q_B=10$$ Putting this into FOC equation (3) gives $$100-q_A-10=0 \implies q_A=90$$ Total cost for minimisation: $$TC=TC_A+TC_B=10+20\times 90+2+10^2=1912$$

  2. A manufacturing firm has access to two aging factories, G and H, for manufacturing. The firm does $g$ items from G and $h$ items from H. The costs of the factory production are: $$TC_G=80+10g^{1.5}$$ $$TC_H=100+12h^{1.25}$$ If the firm produces 420 items, what is the cost of manufacturing if:

    1. All are at G?

    2. All are at H?

    3. If they minimise the total cost and use both G and H?

    Show Solution
    1. All are at G: $TC=80+10 \times 420^{1.5} \approx 86154$
    2. All are at H: $TC=100+12 \times 420^{1.25} \approx 22916$

    3. Minimise the total cost and use both G and H

      $TC=80+10g^{1.5}+100+12h^{1.25}$ subject to $g+h=420$

      Lagrangian: $$L=80+10g^{1.5}+100+12h^{1.25}+\lambda(420-g-h)$$

      FOC: \begin{align} L_g&=15g^{0.5}-\lambda=0\\ L_h&=15g^{0.25}-\lambda=0\\ L_{\lambda}&=420-g-h=0 \end{align} Using first two FOC we have: \begin{align} 15g^{0.5}&=\lambda\\ 15h^{0.25}&=\lambda \end{align} Dividing these equations we have: \begin{align} \frac{15g^{0.5}}{15h^{0.25}}&=1\\ g^{0.5}&=h^{0.25}\\ \text{raising to power 4:} \hspace{1cm} g^2&=h \end{align} Substitute for $h$ in the contraint: \begin{align*} 420-g-g^2&=0\\ \implies g^2+g-420&=0\\ \implies (g-20)(g+21)&=0\\ \implies g&=20 \text{ since }g=-21 \text{ is not sensible}\\ \implies h&=400 \end{align*} So, total cost will be: $$TC=80+10\times 20^{1.5}+100+12 \times 400^{1.25} \approx 22451$$

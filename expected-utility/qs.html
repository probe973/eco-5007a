Expected Value and Utility Optimisation

For each question below, find the solution and then reveal the answer.

  1. Find the expected values for the following:

    1. A game pays $75$ with probability $0.4$, $25$ with probability $0.3$, and $10$ with probability $0.3$.

    2. A variable $X$ takes values $5, 10, 15, 20$ with probabilities $0.1, 0.2, 0.4, 0.3$ respectively.

    3. A random process yields $100$ with $20\%$ chance, $20$ with $50\%$ chance, and $-10$ with $30\%$ chance.

    Show Solution

    1. The expected value (EV) is calculated as $EV = \sum x_i P(x_i)$.

      $$ \begin{aligned} EV &= (75 \times 0.4) + (25 \times 0.3) + (10 \times 0.3) \\ &= 30 + 7.5 + 3 \\ &= 40.5 \end{aligned} $$

      The expected value is $40.5$.

    2. $$ \begin{aligned} EV &= (5 \times 0.1) + (10 \times 0.2) + (15 \times 0.4) + (20 \times 0.3) \\ &= 0.5 + 2 + 6 + 6 \\ &= 14.5 \end{aligned} $$

      The expected value is $14.5$.

    3. $$ \begin{aligned} EV &= (100 \times 0.2) + (20 \times 0.5) + (-10 \times 0.3) \\ &= 20 + 10 - 3 \\ &= 27 \end{aligned} $$

      The expected value is $27$.

  2. An investor has current wealth of $\pounds 500$ and utility function $U(w) = w^2$. They invest in a venture. Their total wealth will become $\pounds 600$ with $70\%$ probability, or $\pounds 450$ with $30\%$ probability. Calculate the expected utility of this investment.

    Show Solution

    The investor's utility function is $U(w) = w^2$. Expected utility (EU) is calculated as $EU = \sum U(w_i) P(w_i)$.

    $$ \begin{aligned} EU &= U(600) \times 0.7 + U(450) \times 0.3 \\ &= (600^2 \times 0.7) + (450^2 \times 0.3) \\ &= (360000 \times 0.7) + (202500 \times 0.3) \\ &= 252000 + 60750 \\ &= 312750 \end{aligned} $$

    The expected utility of this investment is $312750$.

  3. For each of the options below, find the option that yields the greatest expected utility:

    1. An individual has current wealth $\pounds 1,000$ and utility function $U(w) = w$. They consider two investment choices:

      1. A secure investment where total wealth becomes $\pounds 1,100$ with $100\%$ certainty.

      2. A risky investment where total wealth becomes $\pounds 1,300$ with $60\%$ probability, or $\pounds 900$ with $40\%$ probability.

    2. A decision-maker has $\pounds 500$ in current capital and utility function $U(c) = c^2$. They evaluate two project proposals:

      1. Project Alpha: Capital remains $\pounds 500$ with $100\%$ certainty.

      2. Project Beta: Capital becomes $\pounds 700$ with $25\%$ probability, or $\pounds 400$ with $75\%$ probability.

    3. A firm with $\pounds 10,000$ in assets has utility function $U(a) = \sqrt{a}$. They face a $10\%$ chance of a $\pounds 2,000$ loss due to an event. They can either:

      1. Accept the risk without protection.

      2. Purchase a protection plan for a premium of $\pounds 150$ that fully covers the $\pounds 2,000$ loss if the event occurs.

    4. A policy maker with initial support score of $100$ has utility function $U(s) = \ln(s)$. They are choosing between two policies:

      1. Policy X: Guarantees a support score of $110$.

      2. Policy Y: Results in a support score of $130$ with $50\%$ probability, or $95$ with $50\%$ probability.

    Show Solution

    1. Initial wealth is $\pounds 1,000$. Utility function is $U(w) = w$.

      1. Secure Investment: Wealth becomes $\pounds 1,100$ with $100\%$ certainty.

        $$ \begin{aligned} EU_i &= U(1100) \times 1 \\ &= 1100 \times 1 \\ &= 1100 \end{aligned} $$

      2. Risky Investment: Wealth becomes $\pounds 1,300$ with $60\%$ prob, or $\pounds 900$ with $40\%$ prob.

        $$ \begin{aligned} EU_{ii} &= U(1300) \times 0.6 + U(900) \times 0.4 \\ &= 780 + 360 \\ &= 1140 \end{aligned} $$

      Comparing $EU_i = 1100$ and $EU_{ii} = 1140$, the risky investment (ii) yields the greater expected utility.

    2. Current capital is $\pounds 500$. Utility function is $U(c) = c^2$.

      1. Project Alpha: Capital remains $\pounds 500$ with $100\%$ certainty.

        $$ \begin{aligned} EU_i &= U(500) \times 1 \\ &= 500^2 \times 1 \\ &= 250000 \end{aligned} $$

      2. Project Beta: Capital becomes $\pounds 700$ with $25\%$ prob, or $\pounds 400$ with $75\%$ prob.

        $$ \begin{aligned} EU_{ii} &= U(700) \times 0.25 + U(400) \times 0.75 \\ &= (700^2 \times 0.25) + (400^2 \times 0.75) \\ &= 122500 + 120000 \\ &= 242500 \end{aligned} $$

      Comparing $EU_i = 250000$ and $EU_{ii} = 242500$, Project Alpha (i) yields the greater expected utility.

    3. Current assets are $\pounds 10,000$. Utility function is $U(a) = \sqrt{a}$. Probability of $\pounds 2,000$ loss is $10\%$.

      1. Accept the risk without protection.

        $$ \begin{aligned} EU_i &= U(10000) \times 0.9 + U(10000-2000) \times 0.1 \\ &= (10000)^{0.5} \times 0.9 + (8000)^{0.5} \times 0.1 \\ &\approx (100 \times 0.9) + (89.4427 \times 0.1) \\ &= 90 + 8.94427 \\ &= 98.94427 \end{aligned} $$

      2. Purchase protection plan for $\pounds 150$: Loss is fully covered.

        $$ \begin{aligned} EU_{ii} &= U(10000-150) \times 1 \\ &= (9850)^{0.5} \\ &= 99.24716 \end{aligned} $$

      Comparing $EU_i = 98.94427$ and $EU_{ii} = 99.24716$, purchasing the protection plan (ii) yields the greater expected utility.

    4. Initial support score is $100$. Utility function is $U(s) = \ln(s)$.

      1. Policy X: Guarantees a support score of $110$.

        $$ \begin{aligned} EU_i &= U(110) \times 1 \\ &= \ln(110) \\ &= 4.70048 \end{aligned} $$

      2. Policy Y: Results in $130$ with $50\%$ prob, or $95$ with $50\%$ prob.

        $$ \begin{aligned} EU_{ii} &= U(130) \times 0.5 + U(95) \times 0.5 \\ &= (\ln(130) \times 0.5) + (\ln(95) \times 0.5) \\ &\approx 2.433765 + 2.27694 \\ &= 4.710705 \end{aligned} $$

      Comparing $EU_i = 4.70048$ and $EU_{ii} = 4.710705$, Policy Y (ii) yields the greater expected utility.

  4. An individual has just purchased an asset worth $\pounds 100,000$. If a specific adverse event occurs, the asset's value would drop to $\pounds 20,000$. The probability of this event is $5\%$. An insurance company offers to pay $\pounds x$ in the event of the adverse event, if the individual pays an insurance premium of $\pounds 0.08x$. The premium must be paid regardless of whether the event occurs. The individual's utility function is $U(\text{wealth}) = (\text{wealth})^{0.5}$.

    1. Write down the individual's expected utility if they buy insurance coverage of $\pounds x$.

    2. How much insurance (value of $x$) will the individual buy to maximize their expected utility, and what will their insurance premium be?

    Show Solution

    Initial asset value: $\pounds 100,000$. Loss: $\pounds 80,000$ (to $\pounds 20,000$).
    Probability of adverse event: $p_L = 0.05$. Coverage: $\pounds x$. Premium: $\pounds 0.08x$.
    Utility function: $U(\text{wealth}) = (\text{wealth})^{0.5}$.

    1. Expected utility if they buy insurance coverage of $\pounds x$:

      • If no adverse event (prob $0.95$):
        Wealth $= 100,000 - 0.08x$
      • If adverse event (prob $0.05$):
        Wealth $= 100,000 - 80,000 - 0.08x + x = 20,000 + 0.92x$

      So, the expected utility $EU(x)$ is:

      $$ EU(x) = 0.95 (100,000 - 0.08x)^{0.5} + 0.05 (20,000 + 0.92x)^{0.5} $$

    2. To maximize expected utility, we take the derivative of $EU(x)$ with respect to $x$ and set it to zero.

      $$ \begin{aligned} \frac{dEU}{dx} &= 0.95 \times 0.5 (100,000 - 0.08x)^{-0.5} (-0.08) \\ &\quad + 0.05 \times 0.5 (20,000 + 0.92x)^{-0.5} (0.92) = 0 \\[1em] \frac{-0.038}{ (100,000 - 0.08x)^{0.5}} &+ \frac{0.023}{ (20,000 + 0.92x)^{0.5}} = 0 \\ \frac{0.023}{ (20,000 + 0.92x)^{0.5}} &= \frac{0.038}{ (100,000 - 0.08x)^{0.5}} \end{aligned} $$

      Cross-multiply and square both sides:

      $$ \begin{aligned} 0.023 (100,000 - 0.08x)^{0.5} &= 0.038 (20,000 + 0.92x)^{0.5} \\ (0.023)^2 (100,000 - 0.08x) &= (0.038)^2 (20,000 + 0.92x) \\ 0.000529 (100,000 - 0.08x) &= 0.001444 (20,000 + 0.92x) \\ 52.9 - 0.00004232x &= 28.88 + 0.00132848x \\ 52.9 - 28.88 &= 0.00132848x + 0.00004232x \\ 24.02 &= 0.0013708x \\ x &= \frac{24.02}{0.0013708} \approx 17522.61 \end{aligned} $$

      The optimal insurance coverage is $\mathbf{\pounds 17,522.61}$.

      The insurance premium will be $0.08x$:

      $$ \text{Premium} = 0.08 \times 17522.61 \approx \mathbf{\pounds 1,401.81} $$

  5. An investor has $\pounds 10,000$. They can invest an amount $\pounds x$ in a new project. With $60\%$ probability, the project pays back $2.5x$ (a gain of $1.5x$). With $40\%$ probability, the project results in a loss of $0.5x$ (meaning $0.5x$ is returned, $0.5x$ is lost). The money not invested remains unchanged. The investor's utility function is $U(\text{wealth}) = (\text{wealth})^{0.75}$.

    1. Express the investor's expected utility as a function of the amount $\pounds x$ invested.

    2. Determine the optimal amount $\pounds x$ that maximizes their expected utility.

    Show Solution

    Initial capital: $\pounds 10,000$. Investment amount: $\pounds x$.
    Utility function: $U(\text{capital}) = (\text{capital})^{0.75}$.

    1. Express expected utility as a function of $x$:

      • Success (prob $0.6$): Capital $= (10,000 - x) + 2.5x = 10,000 + 1.5x$
      • Failure (prob $0.4$): Capital $= (10,000 - x) + 0.5x = 10,000 - 0.5x$

      So, the expected utility $EU(x)$ is:

      $$ EU(x) = 0.6 (10,000 + 1.5x)^{0.75} + 0.4 (10,000 - 0.5x)^{0.75} $$

    2. To find the optimal $x$, take the derivative of $EU(x)$ with respect to $x$ and set to zero.

      $$ \begin{aligned} \frac{dEU}{dx} &= 0.6 \times 0.75 (10,000 + 1.5x)^{-0.25} (1.5) \\ &\quad + 0.4 \times 0.75 (10,000 - 0.5x)^{-0.25} (-0.5) = 0 \\[1em] 0.675 (10,000 + 1.5x)^{-0.25} &- 0.15 (10,000 - 0.5x)^{-0.25} = 0 \\ 0.675 (10,000 + 1.5x)^{-0.25} &= 0.15 (10,000 - 0.5x)^{-0.25} \\ \frac{0.675}{0.15} &= \frac{(10,000 + 1.5x)^{0.25}}{(10,000 - 0.5x)^{0.25}} \\ 4.5 &= \left( \frac{10,000 + 1.5x}{10,000 - 0.5x} \right)^{0.25} \end{aligned} $$

      Raise both sides to the power of $4$:

      $$ \begin{aligned} 4.5^4 &= \frac{10,000 + 1.5x}{10,000 - 0.5x} \\ 410.0625 &= \frac{10,000 + 1.5x}{10,000 - 0.5x} \\ 410.0625 (10,000 - 0.5x) &= 10,000 + 1.5x \\ 4,100,625 - 205.03125x &= 10,000 + 1.5x \\ 4,090,625 &= 1.5x + 205.03125x \\ 4,090,625 &= 206.53125x \\ x &= \frac{4,090,625}{206.53125} \approx 19806.31 \end{aligned} $$

      The optimal investment amount is approximately $\mathbf{\pounds 19,806.31}$.

  6. A retired individual has $\pounds 50,000$ in savings. Their utility for total savings is $U(\text{savings}) = \ln(\text{savings})$. They are considering investing an amount $\pounds x$ into a particular stock. If the stock performs well (with $70\%$ probability), the invested amount yields a $60\%$ return (i.e., $1.6x$). If it performs poorly (with $30\%$ probability), they lose $40\%$ of the invested amount (i.e., $0.6x$ remains). The rest of their savings are held securely and do not change.

    1. Formulate the individual's expected utility as a function of the investment amount $\pounds x$.

    2. Calculate the amount $\pounds x$ that the individual should invest to maximize their expected utility.

    Show Solution

    Initial savings: $\pounds 50,000$. Investment amount: $\pounds x$.
    Utility function: $U(\text{savings}) = \ln(\text{savings})$.

    1. Formulate expected utility as a function of $x$:

      • Stock performs well (prob $0.7$): Savings $= (50,000 - x) + 1.6x = 50,000 + 0.6x$
      • Stock performs poorly (prob $0.3$): Savings $= (50,000 - x) + 0.6x = 50,000 - 0.4x$

      So, the expected utility $EU(x)$ is:

      $$ EU(x) = 0.7 \ln(50,000 + 0.6x) + 0.3 \ln(50,000 - 0.4x) $$

    2. To find the optimal $x$, take the derivative of $EU(x)$ with respect to $x$ and set to zero.

      $$ \begin{aligned} \frac{dEU}{dx} &= 0.7 \times \frac{0.6}{50,000 + 0.6x} + 0.3 \times \frac{-0.4}{50,000 - 0.4x} = 0 \\[1em] \frac{0.42}{50,000 + 0.6x} - \frac{0.12}{50,000 - 0.4x} &= 0 \\ \frac{0.42}{50,000 + 0.6x} &= \frac{0.12}{50,000 - 0.4x} \end{aligned} $$

      Cross-multiply:

      $$ \begin{aligned} 0.42 (50,000 - 0.4x) &= 0.12 (50,000 + 0.6x) \\ 21,000 - 0.168x &= 6,000 + 0.072x \\ 15,000 &= 0.24x \\ x &= \frac{15,000}{0.24} = 62,500 \end{aligned} $$

      Since the initial savings are $\pounds 50,000$, and we cannot invest more than we have, the optimal investment amount is limited by the budget. Therefore, the optimal investment amount is $\mathbf{\pounds 50,000}$.

  7. A fund manager has a current wealth of $\pounds 2,000,000$. Their utility for wealth is $U(W) = -e^{-0.0000005W}$. They can invest an amount $\pounds x$ (where $x \ge 0$) into a highly volatile asset. This asset yields a return such that the invested $x$ becomes $1.8x$ with $50\%$ probability, or $0.5x$ with $50\%$ probability. The rest of their wealth $(\pounds 2,000,000 - x)$ is kept in a risk-free account.

    1. Write down the fund manager's expected utility as a function of the investment amount $\pounds x$.

    2. Determine the optimal investment amount $\pounds x$ that maximizes their expected utility.

    Show Solution

    Initial wealth: $\pounds 2,000,000$. Investment amount: $\pounds x$.
    Utility function: $U(W) = -e^{-0.0000005W}$.

    1. Write down expected utility as a function of $x$:

      • Asset performs well (prob $0.5$): Wealth $= (2,000,000 - x) + 1.8x = 2,000,000 + 0.8x$
      • Asset performs poorly (prob $0.5$): Wealth $= (2,000,000 - x) + 0.5x = 2,000,000 - 0.5x$

      So, the expected utility $EU(x)$ is:

      $$ \begin{aligned} EU(x) &= 0.5 (-e^{-0.0000005(2,000,000 + 0.8x)}) \\ &\quad + 0.5 (-e^{-0.0000005(2,000,000 - 0.5x)}) \\ EU(x) &= -0.5 e^{-1 - 0.0000004x} - 0.5 e^{-1 + 0.00000025x} \end{aligned} $$

    2. To find the optimal $x$, take the derivative of $EU(x)$ with respect to $x$ and set to zero.

      $$ \begin{aligned} \frac{dEU}{dx} &= -0.5 e^{-1 - 0.0000004x} (-0.0000004) \\ &\quad - 0.5 e^{-1 + 0.00000025x} (0.00000025) = 0 \\[1em] 0.0000002 e^{-1 - 0.0000004x} &= 0.000000125 e^{-1 + 0.00000025x} \\ \frac{e^{-1 - 0.0000004x}}{e^{-1 + 0.00000025x}} &= \frac{0.000000125}{0.0000002} \\ e^{(-1 - 0.0000004x) - (-1 + 0.00000025x)} &= 0.625 \\ e^{-0.00000065x} &= 0.625 \end{aligned} $$

      Take the natural logarithm of both sides:

      $$ \begin{aligned} -0.00000065x &= \ln(0.625) \\ -0.00000065x &= -0.4700036 \\ x &= \frac{-0.4700036}{-0.00000065} \approx 723082.46 \end{aligned} $$

      The optimal investment amount is approximately $\mathbf{\pounds 723,082.46}$.

Partial Derivatives

  1. For each of the following, find: \[ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x}, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y}, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2}, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2}, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y}, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} \]

    1. \(z=5x+2y\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 5, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 2 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 0, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = 0 $$

    2. \(z=5x^3+3y^2+4\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 15x^2, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 6y $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 30x, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = 6 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = 0 $$

    3. \(z=8xy\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 8y, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 8x $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 0, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = 8 $$

    4. \(z=2xy^{0.5}\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 2y^{0.5}, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = xy^{-0.5} $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 0, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = -0.5xy^{-1.5} $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = y^{-0.5} $$

    5. \(z=e^{2x}+4e^y\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 2e^{2x}, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 4e^y $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 4e^{2x}, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = 4e^y $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = 0 $$

    6. \(z=x^2+2xy+y^2\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 2x+2y, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 2x+2y $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 2, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = 2 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = 2 $$

    7. \(z=10x^2y^3-2x-4y\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 20xy^3-2, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 30x^2y^2-4 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 20y^3, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = 60x^2y $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = 60xy^2 $$

    8. \(z=5x^2+2x+5y^3-2y^2\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 10x+2, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 15y^2-4y $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 10, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = 30y-4 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = 0 $$

    9. \(z=10x^4y^3\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 40x^3y^3, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 30x^4y^2 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 120x^2y^3, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = 60x^4y $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = 120x^3y^2 $$

    10. \(z=2e^{5x}-4x^2y^2+\ln{(3xy)}\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 10e^{5x} - 8xy^2 + \frac{1}{x}, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = -8x^2y + \frac{1}{y} $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 50e^{5x} - 8y^2 - \frac{1}{x^2}, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = -8x^2 - \frac{1}{y^2} $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = -16xy $$

    11. \(z=x^3 \ln{y}\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 3x^2\ln{y}, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = \frac{x^3}{y} $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 6x\ln{y}, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = -\frac{x^3}{y^2} $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = \frac{3x^2}{y} $$

    12. \(z=y(5-2x)\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = -2y, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 5-2x $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 0, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = -2 $$

    13. \(z=y(5-2x)^2\)

      Show Solution
      $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = -4y(5-2x), \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = (5-2x)^2 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x^2} = 8y, \quad \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y^2} = 0 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 z}{\partial y \partial x} = -4(5-2x) $$

  2. Given the demand function: \(Q=50-2P+0.5I\), where \(Q\) is quantity demanded, \(P\) is price, and \(I\) is the income level.

    1. Find \(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial P}\) and \(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial I}\).
    2. Interpret the meanings of these partial derivatives.
    3. Suppose income changes by 100, what is the change in demand?

    Show Solution
    1. $$ \frac{\partial Q}{\partial P} = -2, \quad \frac{\partial Q}{\partial I} = 0.5 $$
    2. \(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial P} = -2\) means that for every 1 unit increase in price \(P\), quantity demanded \(Q\) decreases by 2 units, holding income constant.
      \(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial I} = 0.5\) means that for every 1 unit increase in income \(I\), quantity demanded \(Q\) increases by 0.5 units, holding price constant.
    3. The change in demand is: \( \Delta Q = \frac{\partial Q}{\partial I} \times \Delta I = 0.5 \times 100 = 50 \). Demand increases by 50 units.

  3. Given \(Q(L,K)=AL^{\alpha}K^{1-\alpha}\), where \(Q\) is output, \(L\) is labour, and \(K\) is capital.

    1. Find the partial derivatives \(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial L}\) and \(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial K}\).
    2. Let \(A = 1\) and \(\alpha = 0.3\). Evaluate your expressions at \(L = 10\), \(K = 5\).
    3. Repeat for \(L = 20\), \(K = 5\). How does the value of \(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial L}\) compare to before? Explain your answer using the formula from part (a).

    Show Solution
    1. $$ \frac{\partial Q}{\partial L} = A \alpha L^{\alpha - 1} K^{1 - \alpha}, \quad \frac{\partial Q}{\partial K} = A (1 - \alpha) L^{\alpha} K^{-\alpha} $$
    2. At \(L=10, K=5\): $$ \frac{\partial Q}{\partial L} = 1 \cdot 0.3 \cdot 10^{-0.7} \cdot 5^{0.7} \approx 0.185 $$ $$ \frac{\partial Q}{\partial K} = 1 \cdot 0.7 \cdot 10^{0.3} \cdot 5^{-0.3} \approx 0.862 $$
    3. At \(L=20, K=5\): $$ \frac{\partial Q}{\partial L} = 0.3 \cdot 20^{-0.7} \cdot 5^{0.7} \approx 0.113 $$ The value of \(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial L}\) (the marginal product of labour) has decreased. This is because the exponent on \(L\) in the formula for \(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial L}\) is \(\alpha - 1 = -0.7\), which is negative. Therefore, as \(L\) increases, \(L^{\alpha-1}\) decreases, demonstrating the principle of diminishing marginal returns to labour.

First Order Conditions

  1. For each of the functions \(f(x,y)\) below, find:

    1. \(f_x\) and \(f_y\)
    2. the values of \(x\), \(y\), and \(f(x,y)\) where both \(f_x=0\) and \(f_y=0\)

    1. \(f(x,y)=x^2+y^2\)

      Show Solution

      (i) \( f_x = 2x, \quad f_y = 2y \)

      (ii) Setting \(f_x = 0 \Rightarrow x=0\). Setting \(f_y = 0 \Rightarrow y=0\).

      The point is \((0,0)\), and \(f(0,0) = 0^2+0^2=0\).

    2. \(f(x,y)=x^2-2x-y^2-4y-3\)

      Show Solution

      (i) \( f_x = 2x - 2, \quad f_y = -2y - 4 \)

      (ii) Setting \(2x-2=0 \Rightarrow x=1\). Setting \(-2y-4=0 \Rightarrow y=-2\).

      The point is \((1, -2)\), and \(f(1,-2) = 1^2 - 2(1) - (-2)^2 - 4(-2) - 3 = 1 - 2 - 4 + 8 - 3 = 0\).

    3. \(f(x,y)=x^2y-2xy^2+3xy+4\)

      Show Solution

      Step 1: Compute the partial derivatives

      $$ f_x = 2xy - 2y^2 + 3y $$ $$ f_y = x^2 - 4xy + 3x $$

      Step 2: Set derivatives to zero and factor

      $$ y(2x - 2y + 3) = 0 \quad (1) $$ $$ x(x - 4y + 3) = 0 \quad (2) $$

      Step 3: Solve the system by cases

      Case 1: \(y = 0\)

      If \(y=0\), equation (1) is satisfied. Substitute \(y=0\) into equation (2):

      $$ x(x - 4(0) + 3) = 0 \implies x(x+3) = 0 $$

      This gives two solutions: \(x=0\) or \(x=-3\). This leads to two critical points: \((0, 0)\) and \((-3, 0)\).

      Case 2: \(x = 0\)

      If \(x=0\), equation (2) is satisfied. Substitute \(x=0\) into equation (1):

      $$ y(2(0) - 2y + 3) = 0 \implies y(-2y+3) = 0 $$

      This gives two solutions: \(y=0\) or \(y=\frac{3}{2}\). This leads to two critical points: \((0, 0)\) and \((0, \frac{3}{2})\).

      Case 3: \(2x - 2y + 3 = 0\) and \(x - 4y + 3 = 0\)

      Here we solve the system of linear equations. From the second equation, we can express \(x\):

      $$ x = 4y - 3 $$

      Substitute this into the first equation:

      $$ 2(4y - 3) - 2y + 3 = 0 $$ $$ 8y - 6 - 2y + 3 = 0 $$ $$ 6y - 3 = 0 \implies y = \frac{1}{2} $$

      Now find the corresponding value for \(x\):

      $$ x = 4\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) - 3 = 2 - 3 = -1 $$

      This gives the critical point \((-1, \frac{1}{2})\).

      Step 4: List points and evaluate the function

      Combining all cases, we have four unique critical points. Now we evaluate \(f(x,y)\) at each:

      • At \((0, 0)\): \(f(0, 0) = 4\)
      • At \((-3, 0)\): \(f(-3, 0) = (-3)^2(0) - 2(-3)(0)^2 + 3(-3)(0) + 4 = 4\)
      • At \((0, \frac{3}{2})\): \(f(0, \frac{3}{2}) = (0)^2(\frac{3}{2}) - 2(0)(\frac{3}{2})^2 + 3(0)(\frac{3}{2}) + 4 = 4\)
      • At \((-1, \frac{1}{2})\): $$ f\left(-1, \frac{1}{2}\right) = (-1)^2\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) - 2(-1)\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 + 3(-1)\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) + 4 $$ $$ = \frac{1}{2} - 2(-1)\left(\frac{1}{4}\right) - \frac{3}{2} + 4 $$ $$ = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{2}{4} - \frac{3}{2} + 4 = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2} - \frac{3}{2} + 4 = 1 - \frac{3}{2} + 4 = 3.5 $$

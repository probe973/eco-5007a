Skills - Rearranging Equations

In each of the following expressions, make $x$ the subject of the equation.

  1. $x + y = 12$

    Show Solution

    Subtract $y$ from both sides:

    $$ x = 12 - y $$

  2. $5x - 4 = y$

    Show Solution

    Add $4$ to both sides:

    $$ 5x = y + 4 $$

    Divide by $5$:

    $$ x = \frac{y + 4}{5} $$

  3. $ax - 3by = 9$

    Show Solution

    Add $3by$ to both sides:

    $$ ax = 9 + 3by $$

    Divide by $a$:

    $$ x = \frac{9 + 3by}{a} $$

  4. $y = mx + c$

    Show Solution

    Subtract $c$ from both sides:

    $$ y - c = mx $$

    Divide by $m$:

    $$ x = \frac{y - c}{m} $$

  5. $2 + 5xy = 6$

    Show Solution

    Subtract $2$ from both sides:

    $$ 5xy = 6 - 2 $$ $$ 5xy = 4 $$

    Divide by $5y$:

    $$ x = \frac{4}{5y} $$

  6. $9x + 6y = 4x + 1$

    Show Solution

    Subtract $4x$ from both sides:

    $$ 9x - 4x + 6y = 1 $$ $$ 5x + 6y = 1 $$

    Subtract $6y$ from both sides:

    $$ 5x = 1 - 6y $$

    Divide by $5$:

    $$ x = \frac{1 - 6y}{5} $$

Tip: Gather all terms containing $x$ on one side of the equation and all other terms on the opposite side. Then, factor out $x$.

  1. $ax + by = 4x + 1$

    Show Solution

    Subtract $4x$ from both sides:

    $$ ax - 4x + by = 1 $$

    Subtract $by$ from both sides:

    $$ ax - 4x = 1 - by $$

    Factor out $x$:

    $$ x(a - 4) = 1 - by $$

    Divide by $(a - 4)$:

    $$ x = \frac{1 - by}{a - 4} $$

  2. $ax - by = bx - 8$

    Show Solution

    Subtract $bx$ from both sides:

    $$ ax - bx - by = -8 $$

    Add $by$ to both sides:

    $$ ax - bx = by - 8 $$

    Factor out $x$:

    $$ x(a - b) = by - 8 $$

    Divide by $(a - b)$:

    $$ x = \frac{by - 8}{a - b} $$

  3. $\dfrac{2ty + mx}{ty - mx} = 1$

    Show Solution

    Multiply both sides by $(ty - mx)$:

    $$ 2ty + mx = 1 \times (ty - mx) $$ $$ 2ty + mx = ty - mx $$

    Add $mx$ to both sides:

    $$ 2ty + 2mx = ty $$

    Subtract $2ty$ from both sides:

    $$ 2mx = -ty $$

    Divide by $2m$:

    $$ x = \frac{-ty}{2m} $$

  4. $\dfrac{ty + mx}{ty - 3mx} = k$

    Show Solution

    Multiply both sides by $(ty - 3mx)$:

    $$ ty + mx = k(ty - 3mx) $$ $$ ty + mx = kty - 3kmx $$

    Subtract $mx$ from both sides:

    $$ ty = kty - 3kmx - mx $$

    Subtract $kty$ from both sides:

    $$ ty - kty = -3kmx - mx $$

    Factor out $ty$ on the left and $-x$ on the right:

    $$ ty(1 - k) = -x(3km + m) $$ $$ ty(1 - k) = -x m(3k + 1) $$

    Divide by $-m(3k + 1)$:

    $$ x = \frac{ty(1 - k)}{-m(3k + 1)} $$ $$ x = \frac{ty(k - 1)}{m(3k + 1)} \quad \text{(multiplying top and bottom by -1 for cleaner form)} $$

  5. $9x^2 = 4y$

    Show Solution

    Divide by $9$:

    $$ x^2 = \frac{4y}{9} $$

    Take the square root of both sides:

    $$ x = \pm\sqrt{\frac{4y}{9}} $$ $$ x = \pm\frac{\sqrt{4}\sqrt{y}}{\sqrt{9}} $$ $$ x = \pm\frac{2\sqrt{y}}{3} $$

  6. $x^3 - 5m = t$

    Show Solution

    Add $5m$ to both sides:

    $$ x^3 = t + 5m $$

    Take the cube root of both sides:

    $$ x = \sqrt[3]{t + 5m} \quad \text{or} \quad x = (t + 5m)^\frac{1}{3} $$

  7. $x^2 - 2x = 15$

    Show Solution

    Subtract $15$ from both sides to get quadratic form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$:

    $$ x^2 - 2x - 15 = 0 $$

    Factor the quadratic:

    $$ (x - 5)(x + 3) = 0 $$

    Set each factor to zero:

    $$ x - 5 = 0 \implies x = 5 $$ $$ x + 3 = 0 \implies x = -3 $$

    So, $x = 5$ or $x = -3$.

Tip: Rearrange this into the standard quadratic form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ and then consider using the quadratic formula or factoring.

  1. $x^2 = 2 - qx$

    Show Solution

    Rearrange to $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$:

    $$ x^2 + qx - 2 = 0 $$

    Using the quadratic formula $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$ with $a=1, b=q, c=-2$:

    $$ x = \frac{-q \pm \sqrt{q^2 - 4(1)(-2)}}{2(1)} $$ $$ x = \frac{-q \pm \sqrt{q^2 + 8}}{2} $$

  2. $x^2 - 8 = kx$

    Show Solution

    Rearrange to $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$:

    $$ x^2 - kx - 8 = 0 $$

    Using the quadratic formula $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$ with $a=1, b=-k, c=-8$:

    $$ x = \frac{-(-k) \pm \sqrt{(-k)^2 - 4(1)(-8)}}{2(1)} $$ $$ x = \frac{k \pm \sqrt{k^2 + 32}}{2} $$

Rearranging with Indices

Recall the rules of indices:

$$ \begin{align*} x^ax^b&=x^{a+b}\\ \frac{x^a}{x^b}&=x^{a-b}\\ (x^a)^b&=x^{ab} \end{align*} $$

It is also useful to note that a number multiplied by its reciprocal is 1, e.g. $\frac{3}{4}\times \frac{4}{3}=1$

Examples:

Arrange to make $x$ the subject of $x^\frac{4}{9} = y^\frac{1}{3}$

Solution:

$$ \begin{align*} x^{\frac{4}{9}\times \frac{9}{4}}&=y^{\frac{1}{3}\times \frac{9}{4}}\\ x&=y^\frac{3}{4} \end{align*} $$

Arrange to make $x$ the subject of $x^{\frac{2}{3}}-y=10$

Solution:

$$ \begin{align*} x^\frac{2}{3}&=10+y\\ x&=(10+y)^\frac{3}{2} \end{align*} $$

Practice Problems:

Rearrange the following to make $x$ the subject:

  1. $x^{\frac{3}{4}} = y^{\frac{1}{2}}$

    Show Solution

    Raise both sides to the power of $\frac{4}{3}$:

    $$ (x^{\frac{3}{4}})^{\frac{4}{3}} = (y^{\frac{1}{2}})^{\frac{4}{3}} $$ $$ x = y^{\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{4}{3}} $$ $$ x = y^{\frac{4}{6}} $$ $$ x = y^{\frac{2}{3}} $$

  2. $x^{\frac{3}{4}} = y^{\frac{1}{2}} - 2$

    Show Solution

    Raise both sides to the power of $\frac{4}{3}$:

    $$ (x^{\frac{3}{4}})^{\frac{4}{3}} = (y^{\frac{1}{2}} - 2)^{\frac{4}{3}} $$ $$ x = (y^{\frac{1}{2}} - 2)^{\frac{4}{3}} $$

  3. $5x^8 - y = 0$

    Show Solution

    Add $y$ to both sides:

    $$ 5x^8 = y $$

    Divide by $5$:

    $$ x^8 = \frac{y}{5} $$

    Take the $8^{\text{th}}$ root of both sides:

    $$ x = \pm\left(\frac{y}{5}\right)^{\frac{1}{8}} $$

  4. $x^{\frac{3}{4}} y = 8my$

    Show Solution

    Assuming $y \ne 0$, divide both sides by $y$:

    $$ x^{\frac{3}{4}} = 8m $$

    Raise both sides to the power of $\frac{4}{3}$:

    $$ (x^{\frac{3}{4}})^{\frac{4}{3}} = (8m)^{\frac{4}{3}} $$ $$ x = 8^{\frac{4}{3}} m^{\frac{4}{3}} $$ $$ x = (\sqrt[3]{8})^4 m^{\frac{4}{3}} $$ $$ x = 2^4 m^{\frac{4}{3}} $$ $$ x = 16 m^{\frac{4}{3}} $$

  5. $2x^{2.5} = 64$

    Show Solution

    $2.5 = \frac{5}{2}$

    $$ 2x^{\frac{5}{2}} = 64 $$

    Divide by $2$:

    $$ x^{\frac{5}{2}} = 32 $$

    Raise both sides to the power of $\frac{2}{5}$:

    $$ (x^{\frac{5}{2}})^{\frac{2}{5}} = 32^{\frac{2}{5}} $$ $$ x = (\sqrt[5]{32})^2 $$ $$ x = 2^2 $$ $$ x = 4 $$

  6. $x^2 y^3 x^5 y^2 = a$

    Show Solution

    Group like terms and use $x^ax^b = x^{a+b}$:

    $$ x^{2+5} y^{3+2} = a $$ $$ x^7 y^5 = a $$

    Divide by $y^5$:

    $$ x^7 = \frac{a}{y^5} $$

    Take the $7^{\text{th}}$ root of both sides:

    $$ x = \left(\frac{a}{y^5}\right)^{\frac{1}{7}} \quad \text{or} \quad x = \frac{a^{\frac{1}{7}}}{y^{\frac{5}{7}}} $$

  7. $0.5 x^{-0.5} y^{-0.5} \times 0.5 x^{-0.5} y^{0.5} = 4$

    Show Solution

    Multiply the numerical coefficients and group variables:

    $$ (0.5 \times 0.5) \times (x^{-0.5} x^{-0.5}) \times (y^{-0.5} y^{0.5}) = 4 $$ $$ 0.25 \times x^{(-0.5) + (-0.5)} \times y^{(-0.5) + (0.5)} = 4 $$ $$ 0.25 x^{-1} y^0 = 4 $$

    Since $y^0 = 1$ (for $y \ne 0$):

    $$ 0.25 x^{-1} = 4 $$ $$ 0.25 \times \frac{1}{x} = 4 $$ $$ \frac{0.25}{x} = 4 $$

    Multiply by $x$:

    $$ 0.25 = 4x $$

    Divide by $4$:

    $$ x = \frac{0.25}{4} $$ $$ x = \frac{1/4}{4} $$ $$ x = \frac{1}{16} $$

  8. $0.8 y^{-0.2} x^{0.2} \times 0.2 y^{0.8} x^{-0.8} = 10$

    Show Solution

    Group numerical coefficients and variables:

    $$ (0.8 \times 0.2) \times (y^{-0.2} y^{0.8}) \times (x^{0.2} x^{-0.8}) = 10 $$ $$ 0.16 \times y^{0.6} \times x^{-0.6} = 10 $$ $$ 0.16 y^{0.6} \frac{1}{x^{0.6}} = 10 $$ $$ \frac{0.16 y^{0.6}}{x^{0.6}} = 10 $$

    Multiply by $x^{0.6}$:

    $$ 0.16 y^{0.6} = 10 x^{0.6} $$

    Divide by $10$:

    $$ \frac{0.16 y^{0.6}}{10} = x^{0.6} $$ $$ 0.016 y^{0.6} = x^{0.6} $$

    Raise both sides to the power of $\frac{1}{0.6}$ (or $\frac{5}{3}$):

    $$ (0.016 y^{0.6})^{\frac{1}{0.6}} = (x^{0.6})^{\frac{1}{0.6}} $$ $$ x = (0.016)^{\frac{1}{0.6}} (y^{0.6})^{\frac{1}{0.6}} $$ $$ x = (0.016)^{\frac{5}{3}} y $$ $$ x \approx 0.0883 y $$

  9. $\dfrac{\alpha y^{\alpha - 1} x^{\beta}}{\beta y^{\alpha} x^{\beta - 1}} = k$

    Show Solution

    Separate terms and use $\frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b}$:

    $$ \frac{\alpha}{\beta} \times \frac{y^{\alpha - 1}}{y^{\alpha}} \times \frac{x^{\beta}}{x^{\beta - 1}} = k $$ $$ \frac{\alpha}{\beta} \times y^{(\alpha - 1) - \alpha} \times x^{\beta - (\beta - 1)} = k $$ $$ \frac{\alpha}{\beta} \times y^{-1} \times x^{1} = k $$ $$ \frac{\alpha}{\beta} \times \frac{1}{y} \times x = k $$ $$ \frac{\alpha x}{\beta y} = k $$

    Multiply by $\beta y$:

    $$ \alpha x = k \beta y $$

    Divide by $\alpha$:

    $$ x = \frac{k \beta y}{\alpha} $$
